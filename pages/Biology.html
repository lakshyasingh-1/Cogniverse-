Biology Timeline

Discover how humanity understood life, evolution, cells, genetics, ecosystems, and modern biological sciences.

Ancient Biology

3000 BCE – 400 BCE

Ancient civilizations studied plants, animals, and medicine. Egyptians and Greeks documented anatomy and medicinal herbs.

Greek Natural Philosophy

400 BCE – 200 BCE

Aristotle classified living organisms and laid the foundations of zoology and biological observation.

Microscope Revolution

1600 CE – 1700 CE

Scientists like Robert Hooke and Antonie van Leeuwenhoek discovered cells and microorganisms using microscopes.

Evolutionary Biology

1800 CE – 1900 CE

Charles Darwin introduced the theory of evolution by natural selection, transforming biological science forever.

Modern Biology

1900 CE – Present

Biology now includes genetics, biotechnology, DNA research, neuroscience, medicine, and environmental science.
