Mathematics Timeline

Discover how mathematics evolved through civilizations and transformed science, technology, astronomy, engineering, and modern computing.

Ancient Mathematics

3000 BCE – 500 BCE

Egyptians and Babylonians developed arithmetic, geometry, calendars, and measurement systems for construction and trade.

Greek Mathematics

500 BCE – 300 BCE

Pythagoras, Euclid, and Archimedes established logical proofs, geometry, and mathematical reasoning that shaped future mathematics.

Islamic Golden Age

800 CE – 1400 CE

Al-Khwarizmi introduced algebra and algorithms while Islamic scholars preserved and expanded mathematical knowledge.

Calculus Revolution

1600 CE – 1700 CE

Newton and Leibniz independently developed calculus, revolutionizing physics, engineering, and astronomy.

Modern Mathematics

1900 CE – Present

Mathematics now powers artificial intelligence, cryptography, quantum computing, statistics, and advanced scientific research.
