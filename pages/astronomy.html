The Evolution of Astronomy

Explore the history of astronomy from ancient stargazing civilizations to telescopes, galaxies, black holes, and the modern exploration of the universe.

Explore Timeline

Astronomy Timeline

Discover how humanity understood stars, planets, galaxies, gravity, and the mysteries of the cosmos through history.

Ancient Stargazing

3000 BCE – 500 BCE

Ancient Egyptians, Babylonians, Indians, and Mayans observed stars, created calendars, and tracked planetary movements.

Greek Astronomy

500 BCE – 200 CE

Greek astronomers proposed models of the cosmos and studied planetary motion using mathematics and observation.

Telescope Revolution

1600 CE – 1700 CE

Galileo used telescopes to observe moons, planets, and celestial bodies, transforming astronomy forever.

Modern Astronomy

1800 CE – 1900 CE

Scientists discovered galaxies, nebulae, spectroscopy, and the large-scale structure of the universe.

Space Age

1900 CE – Present

Space telescopes, satellites, and interplanetary missions revealed black holes, exoplanets, and cosmic evolution.
